      <p>There's cleaning — and then there's deep cleaning. Regular maintenance keeps your home looking tidy, but deep cleaning addresses the grime, allergens, and bacteria that accumulate in the places your regular routine misses. In a NYC apartment, where space is tight and air circulation can be limited, deep cleaning is especially important.</p>

      <h2>What's the Difference Between Regular Cleaning and Deep Cleaning?</h2>
      <p>Regular cleaning covers the surfaces you see every day: wiping counters, vacuuming floors, cleaning the bathroom. Deep cleaning goes further — inside appliances, behind furniture, under beds, scrubbing grout, cleaning light fixtures, and addressing every area that doesn't get attention in a standard clean.</p>

      <h2>The General Rule: Twice a Year</h2>
      <p>For most households, a professional deep clean twice a year — typically spring and fall — is a solid foundation. These seasonal resets clear out accumulated dust, allergens, and grime before winter sealing traps everything indoors, and again before you open the windows in spring.</p>

      <h2>When You Need More Frequent Deep Cleaning</h2>

      <h3>Homes with Pets</h3>
      <p>Pet owners should deep clean every 3-4 months. Pet dander, hair, and tracked-in debris accumulate quickly and deeply in carpets and upholstery. Without regular deep cleaning, allergen levels in pet households can become significant even for non-allergic residents.</p>

      <h3>Homes with Young Children</h3>
      <p>If you have children under 5, deep clean quarterly. Young children spend more time on floors and put hands in mouths, making thorough cleaning of floor surfaces, play areas, and frequently touched objects especially important.</p>

      <h3>Allergy Sufferers</h3>
      <p>If anyone in your household has allergies or asthma, deep cleaning every 2-3 months can significantly reduce symptoms. Focus particularly on carpets, bedding areas, and HVAC vents.</p>

      <h3>High-Traffic NYC Apartments</h3>
      <p>NYC apartments get more foot traffic, and more street grime tracked inside, than suburban homes. If you entertain frequently or have a busy household, quarterly deep cleans may be appropriate.</p>

      <h2>Signs Your Home Needs a Deep Clean Now</h2>
      <ul>
        <li>Dust bunnies under furniture or in corners</li>
        <li>Grout in bathrooms or kitchens has darkened</li>
        <li>Refrigerator smells even after wiping surfaces</li>
        <li>Allergy symptoms worsening at home</li>
        <li>After any illness in the household</li>
        <li>Before or after a major event or gathering</li>
        <li>When moving in or moving out</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>What a Professional Deep Clean Covers</h2>
      <p>A professional deep clean is significantly more thorough than what most homeowners do on their own. Sparkle Clean NYC's deep cleaning service covers inside appliances (oven, refrigerator), inside cabinets, baseboards, light fixtures, ceiling fans, window sills and tracks, behind and under heavy furniture, and detailed bathroom scrubbing including grout.</p>
